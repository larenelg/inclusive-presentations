It's not easy!

Our world is very visual focused.

My presentations are rarely 100% inclusive (if that is a thing), I still make mistakes, it's always about learning and doing better with very talk.

Some resources

  • Inclusive Design for Accessible Presentations by Allison Ravenhall
  • Making online conferences more accessible by John Allsopp

As a speaker, p1 of 2

  • Send out the slides, in advance, make sure they are accessible.
  • Try to write the talk where the slides are not critical to the content. If it's not possible, practice describing slides.
  • Leave some room on the bottom for subtitles, and low projectors.

As a speaker, p2 of 2

  • Avoid "sight-only" jokes, e.g. showing a meme without saying anything.
  • Talk slowly and clearly.
  • Make the font as big as possible.

As an organiser, p1 of 3

  • Ask the audience before they event if they have any access requirements, let the speaker know if there's anything they need to be aware of.
  • Ask the speaker if they have any access requirements.

As an organiser, p2 of 3

List of accommodations to consider

  • Accessible venue with accessible toilet.
  • Ramps to the stage.
  • (If online) Automatic captions on Zoom.
  • Microphone for the speaker.

As an organiser, p3 of 3

List of accommodations to consider

  • Large screen above people's heads, within 5-10 meters of audience.
  • Live captioning.
  • Sign language interpreter.
